## Managing dialogues

The Session layer establishes, maintains, synchronizes, and gracefully tears down **sessions** —
ongoing dialogues between two applications. It introduces ideas like **dialog control** (who may send,
and when) and **checkpointing/synchronization** so a long transfer can resume after a failure rather
than restart.

<Callout variant="note" title="A note on the modern stack">
In the real TCP/IP world, the Session layer has **no dedicated protocol**. Its responsibilities are
absorbed by the Transport layer (connections), by application protocols, and by libraries (TLS session
resumption, RPC/gRPC frameworks, WebSocket sessions). We explain both the OSI ideal and where it
actually lives today.
</Callout>
